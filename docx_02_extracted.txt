Step-by-Step Execution Plan for the Revised Hybrid Quantum-Classical Medicinal Plant Health Project
Executive summary
This execution plan turns the revised project into asequenced research programwith hard gates at each stage, so that you do not spend weeks on quantum modeling before the dataset, split policy, and classical baselines are locked down correctly. The central idea from the revised project document is to treat the work as aleakage-safe, species-conditioned, parameter-matched benchmarkrather than a simple â€œCNN plus quantum headâ€ paper. That means the project should advance in the following order: dataset audit and parent-image grouping; leakage-safe grouped splits; backbone and classical baseline lock-in; bottleneck and species-conditioning implementation; VQC head sweeps at 4, 6, and 8 qubits; calibration and statistical testing; then only after that, noise-aware simulation, edge/Green-AI evaluation, robustness, and explainability. The dataset-specific constraints that drive this order are important: the Mendeley dataset contains1,981 original images,20,019 augmented images,seven species, and derived augmented folders, so evaluation can be badly inflated if augmented siblings leak across train and test. îˆ€fileciteîˆ‚turn1file0îˆ
The most efficient way to complete the project is to operate withtwo tiers of experimentation. First, run a fast screening stage with the primary backboneMobileNetV3-Small, grouped splits, and classical/quantum heads on a tiny bottleneck space. Second, expand only the best-performing configurations to additional backbones, finite-shot and noisy simulations, and the deployment-oriented measurements. This is consistent with the revised documentâ€™s emphasis on fair comparisons, parameter matching, careful calibration, and realistic deployment caveats rather than overclaiming quantum advantage. îˆ€fileciteîˆ‚turn1file0îˆ
A practical end state for a strong conference paper is: one held-out grouped test set; grouped 5-fold development cross-validation; classical baselines including logistic regression, SVMs, dense matched heads, and Fourier-feature controls; VQC heads including Ring-SE, ReUpload-Linear, and Local-TTN at 4/6/8 qubits; temperature-scaled calibration; paired statistical tests; latency and energy reporting; then one small noisy-simulation and real-device appendix. A journal version should extend that with robustness, explainability, and preferably a small out-of-distribution or field-style evaluation. îˆ€fileciteîˆ‚turn1file0îˆ
Roadmap and milestone gates
The project is best managed as a set ofmilestone-gated phases. The gate at the end of each phase should be treated as non-negotiable. If a gate fails, the right action is to fix the upstream issue rather than advancing and accumulating technical debt.
